SOLAR CAGE

    Solar Cage is an FPS parkour where you play a janitor robot that works in a dyson sphere facility around the sun, who is suddenly contacted by an unknown force for one objective only: BLOW UP THE SUN. The game is under current development for the summer!

    A total of 9 levels that have the play battle a multittude of enemies along the way, jumping across platforms and dodging obstacles to make it to the end of each level.

    Project Responsibilities:

  • Game Designer, designed and concepted the game.
  • Level Designer, created and iterated all the levels in the game.
  • Gameplay Programmer, programmed an obstacle manager using event actions, wrote the aiming system for the A.I, and other general gameplay components.
  • Technical/Post Effects Artist, used the Shader Node Graph to create shaders in game, such as the sun, enemy bullet effects and more. Used VFX Graph for creating the particle effects in game. Used Unity's post processing effects for camera.

    For the level design of this game, I wanted players to really utilize the wall running/jumping from the character controller, so they can be able to gain high speeds while shooting.

    Enemy tracking was pretty important, so to find a perfect balance, I made the bullets projectiles rather than hitscan/raycast, and the tracking accuracy changes how fast and how close the player is to the enemy.

    Creating the main set piece of the game, the sun, I really wanted to go all out on the shaders for it. Using multiple shader layers that include a distortion effect and as well a disappearing and reappearing noise to make the sun feel 'alive'.

    Using the built in post process effects in Unity, making something like the death screen had to really convey the player was this kind of robot, having an error screen and glitch effect reflect that.
